Labour Force In Malaysia

In 2021, the number of people within the Malaysian Labor Force was approximately 15.67 million. The size of Malaysia's labor force had been increasing in the last ten years. However there have been noticable increases in the Unemployment Rate as well.

Throughout the years three states have had the highest unemployment statistics. These are:

              Selangor | Sabah | Johor

Unemployment Statistics

Unemployment is especially hitting youth, with rates in southeast Asian countries on average three to four times higher than adult Unemployment rates. Part of the problem stems from demographic shifts in the structure of the population and the fact that many of the jobs that formerly employed young people no longer exist.

The Top Countries with the highest unemployment rate are:

1. Brunei
2. Timor
3. Malaysia

Although, Women have increased their educational status and relevant skills in other to attain higher employment standing like their Men counterparts, the supposedly skill improvement has not translated to job improvement and career prospects for women.

Despite significant improvement in cultural, attitudinal and childcare policies in relation to gender gaps from the beginning of the century, female labour participation has been low explaining the relatively Low Unemployment figures for women and Higher Unemployment for men

Labour Force Statistics

Employment by sector separates the working population of the country into three broad group of economic activities: namely the agriculture, industry and services sectors. These three main economic sectors can be disaggregated into additional sub-sectors to provide further details as can be seen HERE.

It is evident that Mining and quarrying along with Wholesale and Retail services are the largest industries with most of the Labour Force concentrated within these sub-sectors.

To meet the economic growth aspirations of the country, the strength, health and skill levels of the workforce are very important determinants. In this context, the education attainment of the workforce has a direct role in shaping the skill level of workers.

Although tertiary education employment has seemingly increased over the years the Average labour force by education remains the same. The Mean remains relatively constant as there is a decrease in the labour force that do not have higher forms of education
